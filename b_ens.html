Entrepreneurship and Start-ups

Venture capital market in Switzerland

New:    Venture capital availability in has been increasing steadily until 2022, but has declined since. The financing rounds have become only slightly fewer, but smaller. The in- and then decreasing VC funding with a peak in 2021/2022 is mirrored in other countries (see below).

Venture capital investments in % of GDP 2017–2024

According to the report «Fast-growing start-ups in Switzerland» (SECO, 2017) the venture capital market in was not as mature as the markets in more advanced countries (such as , the ). The VC market has since expanded, but there is still room for improvement.

The OECD data should be taken with a grain of salt. There is no standard for data collection for VC investement data and there is no single source. The Invest Europe data (p.56), which is most likely the source for the OECD data for Switzerland, does not show an uptick for Switzerland in 2024, but confirms the downward trend in the startupticker.ch data.

Total early-stage entrepreneurial activity (TEA)

The share of 18-64 population who are either a nascent entrepreneur or owner-manager of a new business in has been hovering between 8 and 10 percent. This is a low- to mid-table position among innovation peers.
