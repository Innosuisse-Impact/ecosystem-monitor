A. Research & Development

Funding and expenditure on R&D in Switzerland by sector in 2021

In Switzerland - as in most countries - the majority of R&D funding has its source and its destination in business. The outlow abroad relatively high. The federal government funds R&D mostly indirectly via the universities. A substantial part is the funding of the ETH/EPFL. Innosuisse funding is also a minor part. With the new instruments, the direct funding may be increasing. Israel has very high funding from abroad.

In the following we look in more detail from two perspectives that can be adpoted:

  • Who performs the R&D?
  • Who finances the R&D?

The R&D expenditures of , , and the have increased more significantly, while others have stagnated on levels comparable to Switzerland.

Gross Domestic Expenditure on Research and Development (R&D) in % of GDP 2021

Research and Development in Switzerland is mainly performed (not financed) by private businesses or higher education institutions and rarely by the government itself. Switzerland has one of the lowest share of R&D performed by government institutions. Sweden and Denmark's pattern of research and development most closely mirrors that of Switzerland. The high proportion of R&D carried out by the private sector in Israel is also striking.

Innosuisse's primary instrument – Innovation Projects – contributes to the Higher Education Expenditure on R&D (HERD) since it only covers the research partner's costs while requiring companies to self-fund their portion of the project. In contrast, the newer funding instruments – Swiss Accelerator and Start-up Innovation Projects – would contribute to Business Enterprise Expenditure on R&D (BERD) as they provide direct financial support to companies. However, these programs fall outside the relevant timeframe since they were only recently introduced.
