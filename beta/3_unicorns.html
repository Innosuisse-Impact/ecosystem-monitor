Unicorns

Our report uses CB Insights' unicorn database as our main data source, as it offers the best basis for comparing companies across countries. Since there's no official data on unicorns and different sources define them differently, we compared multiple premium databases like Dealroom, PitchBook, and Crunchbase. While the exact numbers vary, the overall differences between countries are similar.

We've also created our own list of Swiss unicorns, including both current unicorns and former ones that have gone public or been acquired, though some might not meet the strictest definition. Thus the number is significantly higher than the number in the CB Insights data (28 vs 6).

Please note that these statistics should be viewed cautiously, as methods for identifying unicorns differ between data providers.

Number of unicorns relative to GDP

The graph below compares countries' efficiency in creating unicorn companies relative to their economic size, measured as the number of unicorns per $133 billion of GDP. Switzerland produces one unicorn per $133 billion GDP, similar to the Netherlands and Sweden. In contrast, the United States and Israel significantly outperform this benchmark, generating 3-6 times more unicorns relative to their GDP.
