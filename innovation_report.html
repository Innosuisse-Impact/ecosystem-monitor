Innovation Activities in Switzerland

The KOF Swiss Economic Institute of the ETH Zürich publishes every two years the results of its innovation survey. The report and survey are mainly financed by the SEFRI. Since 2019 Innosuisse finances an extension of the company panel with applicants of Innosuisse grants (with data starting from 2016). The results are also included in the Community Innovation Survey of the European Union and allow for limited international comparison (see International Benchmarking: Innovation Activities).

The Swiss innovation landscape: Robust, but facing challenges

The share of companies active in R&D and innovation has been declining for years and has only stabilised in the last survey periods (2016–18 and 2014–16). This mirrors a trend that can be observed internationally.

R&D and innovation are partially becoming decoupled

At the same time, the share of R&D expenditure in turnover and the share of turnover from innovative products and services remained relatively stable, with R&D expenditure rising slightly and the share of innovative products in turnover declining slightly. This means: Fewer companies invest more in R&D. The R&D intensity of the still R&D active companies has increased. And while fewer companies are innovation active, the share of turnover from innovative has only slightly decreased. At the same time the share of non-R&D-driven innovations is growing relatively, indicating a change in innovation strategies.

Productivity is increasing

Survey results show that process innovations are leading to significant reductions in production costs, particularly among SMEs. This is reflected in the development of multifactor productivity (MFP), which points to positive productivity effects from innovation activities. MFP is a measure of economic efficiency that captures the portion of output growth that cannot be explained by increases in labor and capital inputs alone. It reflects how effectively an economy or firm combines its resources to produce goods and services, essentially measuring the "residual" productivity gains that stem from technological progress, improved processes, organizational changes, and other innovations.

Software developers / suppliers have become a source for innovation

Suppliers and customers are key players in companies' innovation processes. Software developers are now the second most important source of knowledge for innovation, with a strong increase in importance. The dependence on international software developers is a risk as well for innovation performance.

Securing funding is no longer the only major obstacle

The high cost of innovation is still the major obstacle to innovation, but hast lost relevance compared to skills shortages and building regulations/spatial planning.

Subnational agencies have gained in importance

Between 4% and 6% of the surveyed Swiss companies have received support from Innosuisse in the observations periods from 2012 to 2022. Since the observation period 2014–16, the share of companies that received support from regional/cantonal agencies has increased from between 2% to 4% to around 7%. The offers from regional agencies have a lower treshhold and are more coaching-oriented than the support offers of Innosuisse.
